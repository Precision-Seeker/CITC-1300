The Ford Model T was introduced to the world in 1908. Henry Ford wanted the Model T to be aFORDable, and simple to use, and also most inportant durable. Due to the mass production of the vehicle Ford Motor Company could sell the vehicle between $260 - $850.

There is also a myth that all Model T's were black. While Henry Ford did say "Any customer can have a car painted any color that he wants as long as it's black" That policy was in place solely for efficiency and uniformity. After 1925 the car was offered in Black, Blue, Red, Grey, and Green.

Ford Model T (1908- 1927)

    1. The Model T was introduced in October 1908
    2. More than 15,000,000 Model T's were built and sold.
    3. The first models were produced at a factory on Piqette Avenue in Detroit.
    4. Henry Ford's initation of mass production of vehicles on the moving assembly line led to lower car prices and to the $5 workday.
